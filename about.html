About Buckeye Laya

Buckeye Laya is Ohio State University’s premier South Asian fusion a cappella competition, celebrating the vibrant intersection of Bollywood, Indian classical, and contemporary Western music. A stage where Desi meets Pitch Perfect, BuckeyeLaya provides a platform for top collegiate a cappella teams to showcase their vocal artistry, creativity, and cultural storytelling in an electrifying competition.

Each year, the competition continues to grow, featuring elite teams that go on to make their mark in prestigious circuits like All-American Awaaz (A3) and ICCA (International Championship of Collegiate A Cappella). Hosted by Dhadkan, Ohio State’s premier South Asian fusion a cappella team, Buckeye Laya stands as a cornerstone of musical excellence, uniting over a hundred passionate performers and fans in a celebration of culture, creativity, and competition.

About ASA

The Association of South-Asian A Cappella is a registered 501(c)(3) non-profit dedicated to championing the unique fusion musical genre of South Asian A Cappella – what we love to refer to as Desi meets Pitch Perfect – a young art form we see as the perfect blend of our South Asian heritage and the Western culture we live around.

Through its pioneering efforts, ASA has played an instrumental role in shaping the collegiate South Asian music landscape across the United States.

In the last decade, South Asian A Cappella has flourished, with our teams consistently making their mark in prestigious competitions like A3 (All-American Awaaz) and ICCA (International Championship of Collegiate A Cappella). We support 85 teams, 8 competitions, and more than 2,000 participants annually across 25 states in the South Asian A Cappella circuit through various initiatives.
